TypeScript gives me a calmer feedback loop. The compiler catches a whole class
of tiny mistakes before they become review comments, runtime bugs, or late-night
debugging sessions.

The part I value most is not strictness for its own sake. It is shared language.
Types describe the shape of a system in a way that stays close to the code, so a
future reader can understand intent without opening five different files.

My usual rule is simple: make important boundaries explicit, keep local code
pleasant to read, and let inference do the boring work.
